Mental Health

It has been shown that Transgender people are more likely to have problems with their mental health due to multiple reasons, some of the reasons are, bullying, discrimination (Transphobia), and gender dysphoria.

    Transgender people are...

  • Six times more likely to have a mood or anxiety disorder
  • Three times as likely to be prescribed antidepressants and antianxiety medications
  • More than six times as likely to attempt or consider suicide
  • More than four times as likely to have an eating disorder

Gender Dysphoria

Gender Dysphoria Gender dysphoria is a term used to describe a sense of uneasiness someone might have because of a disconnection with the gender they were assigned at birth and their gender identity. A example of gender dysphoria is wanting to hide physical signs of your biological sex. Gender Dysphoria can lead to anxiety and depression and can have a harmful impact on everyday life.
