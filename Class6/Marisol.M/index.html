Patsy Mink

Patsy Matsu Takemoto was born on December 6, 1927 in Paia, Hawaii. In 1959 when Hawaii became a U.S. State, Patsy Mink wanted to run for a position in the goverment. Later Patsy Mink became the first woman of color elected to the U.S. House of Representatives and the first Asian-American woman to serve in Congress.
